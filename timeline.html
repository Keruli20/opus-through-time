Baroque Era

The Baroque era was full of drama and detail, with music that sounded rich and busy. Composers like Bach used steady rhythms, smooth melodies and contrasts. Instruments such as the harpsichord, organ and violin were important, and the music was made to impress and move people.

1600-1750

Classical Era

The Classical era was clear and balanced, with music that sounded light, elegant and well-organized. Composers like Mozart and Beethoven wrote pieces with simple melodies, clear structure and changes in mood to keep things interesting. The piano became very important, and the music was meant to be graceful, easy to follow and pleasing to the ear.

1750-1820

Romantic Era

The Romantic era was full of emotion and passion, with music that told stories and expressed deep feelings. Composers like Chopin and Listz wrote powerful melodies and rich harmonies and dramatic contrasts. The music aimed to touch the heart and stir the imagination.

1900-1910

Modernist Era

The Modernist era was experimental and bold, with composers breaking old rules and trying new sounds. Music could be complex or very simple, sometimes using dissonance, unusual rhythms or even silence. Composers like Debussy created dreamy, colorful pieces. Instead of following one style, modernist music was about freedom and creativity.
